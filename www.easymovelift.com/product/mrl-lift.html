Machine Room Less Lift

Machine Room-less Lifts

Machine room-less lifts are elevators that are generally powered by an electric motor and do not require a dedicated lift room for installation. This makes them ideal for locations where available space is limited or when the cost of constructing a lift shaft or room is too high. Machine room-less lifts can be installed in a wide range of locations, including high-rise buildings.

They are generally smaller and more compact than traditional lifts and can be designed to fit almost any floor plan. They are also equipped with safety features, such as sensors, a speed governor, emergency stop buttons, an automatic rescue device with a UPS system, and a buffer to ensure the safety of passengers. In some designs of the machine room-less lifts, the traction machine is placed inside the building shaft and does not require a separate machine room. Machine room-less lifts are also known as Electric lifts.

Purpose Of Machine Room-Less Elevator

The Machine Room-Less (MRL) elevator is a technology that eliminates the need for a separate machinery room in an elevator installation. This technology utilizes a compact and efficient traction machine within the hoistway, freeing up valuable space and reducing the installation cost. The shaft of the machine room-less lifts can be a concrete lift shaft or steel shaft. 

This technology can also reduce noise, vibration, and energy consumption, providing a more efficient and comfortable ride. MRL elevators are ideal for retrofitting existing buildings as well as new construction, offering an efficient and cost-effective solution.

Benefits of Using Machine Room-Less lifts

1. Space Saving: One of the biggest advantages of using machine-room-less (MRL) lifts is that they require significantly less space than traditional lifts. This makes them ideal for buildings with limited space or those with multiple floors, as they do not require the installation of a separate motor room.

2. Cost-effective:Compared to traditional lifts, MRL lifts are often more cost-effective due to their smaller size. This makes them a great choice for businesses working with a tight budget.

3. Safety:MRL lifts are much safer than traditional lifts due to their advanced design and safety features. They are equipped with advanced safety sensors that detect obstacles and are designed to be more resistant to impacts. This type of lift also has an Automatic rescue device with a UPS system, speed governor, Buffer, and limited switches to make them easy to operate.

4. Maintenance:MRL lifts require less maintenance than traditional lifts, as they do not have any moving parts. This means they are less likely to experience breakdowns or malfunctions and require less frequent servicing.

5. Speed:MRL lifts are much faster than traditional lifts, as they are designed to move at speeds of 1m/s to 6 m/s. This makes them the perfect choice for busy buildings or those with a large number of floors.
